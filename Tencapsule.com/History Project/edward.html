An interview with Edward I, King of England from 1272 to his death:

Interviewer: “Hello, I’d like to start by thanking you for all you’ve done for England. Can you tell us some of the secrets to your success?”
Edward I: “I’ve been able to use my experience as a soldier to improve the military forces of our country. Through much perseverance, England has conquered Wales. Simply, you just shouldn’t take no for an answer.”
Int.: “Speaking of perseverance, it has been said you previously ‘forgot danger when [you] wanted revenge.’ You are known to be the greatest monarch of your time, but your attack on the church clergy has brought up questions about how great you are…”
Ed. I: “I admit that I made a poor decision rooted in anger and my temper, but I was forced to take some political power away from the church to keep order and update the English law.”
Int.: “Another point, how do you manage this whole country as well as you do?”
Ed. I: “I make decisions efficiently and quickly, then let my assistants take care of details.”
Int.: “Has the pressure put upon those assistants been a problem?”
Ed. I: “They work hard and have been very reliable to this point.”
Int.: “That brings us to the end of our time. Thank you.”
